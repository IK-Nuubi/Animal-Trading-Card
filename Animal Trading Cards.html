Python

Most Pythons are large snakes that can grow over 30 feet (9 meters) in length.

  • Scientific name: Pythonidae.
  • Average Lifespan: 30 years.
  • Speed: Not more than 1mph.
  • Classification: Carnivore

A cold blooded animal that lives in warm climates. It moves in a straight line by scooting forward, an action which is known as slithering. Pythons are slow animals that cannot go more than 1mph. A Pythons' diet depends on its size; while smaller pythons hunt mostly rodents, lizards and small birds, bigger pythons eat mammals like pigs, antelopes, etc.
